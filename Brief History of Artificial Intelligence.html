Brief History of Artificial Intelligence:

  • 1923 – A Karel Capek play entitled "Rossum's Universal Robots (RUR)" which was premiered in London used the word 'robot' for the first time in London.
  • 1943 – Neutral Network Base Work.
  • 1945 – Isaac Asimov, a Columbia University researcher, creat the term "robotics".

  • 1950 – T'Alan Turing" introduction of 'Turing test' for intelligence research. A groundbreaking paper entitled "Computing Machinery and Intelligence" was also written.

  • 1956 – John McCarthy was the originator of the word "artificial intelligence." At Carnegie Mellon University a prototype of the AI system was attempted.

  • 1958– John McCarthy's Innovation in LISP programming language for AI.

  • 1965 – For the first time at the MIT, Joseph Weizenbaum launched ELIZA, an immersive software that translates messages into English.

  • 1973 – Edinburgh University's Assembly Robotics group developed the invention of popular Scottish robot 'Freddy' which is efficient enough to locate, collect, and build models using vision.

  • 1979 – Stanford Cart, the creator of the first computer-controlled autonomous vehicle.

  • 1985– Harold Cohen created and built a drawing system called 'Aaron'.

  • 1990 – This year important breakthroughs in the AI sector have been found, several of them :
    • Multi-agent planning
    • Usage of Vision by robots
    • Games
    • Virtual Reality
    • Scheduling
    • Normal language comprehension and translation

  • 2000 – MIT featured kismet, a robot with a face capable of exhibiting emotions.The robot Nomad explores the distant areas of the Antarctica and finds meteorites.Robotic pets have been opening up on the market.
